📊 The Complete Data Science Skillset

Data science is the art and science of transforming data into actionable insights. It blends technical mastery with analytical thinking, enabling professionals to uncover patterns, predict outcomes, and drive evidence‑based decisions. At its foundation lie Mathematics & Statistics, powering probability, hypothesis testing, and modeling; the pillars that support all analytical reasoning. These disciplines provide the rigor needed to validate assumptions, quantify uncertainty, and design models that can withstand real‑world complexity. Without this mathematical backbone, even the most advanced algorithms would lack credibility and precision.

Python and R serve as the engines of programming and statistical computing, enabling automation, data manipulation, and advanced analytics. Python’s versatility allows seamless integration of machine learning libraries, data pipelines, and visualization frameworks, while R excels in statistical modeling and exploratory analysis. Together, they empower analysts to move fluidly between raw data and refined insights. SQL ensures efficient data management and retrieval, forming the backbone of structured data operations. Through Data Wrangling and Visualization; using tools like Power BI Power Query, SQL, and R ; raw data is refined, cleaned, and transformed into clear, interactive insights that tell compelling stories. These tools bridge the gap between technical analysis and stakeholder communication, ensuring that insights are not only accurate but also accessible.

Machine Learning expands this foundation with techniques such as supervised and unsupervised learning, regression, classification, clustering, neural networks, and model evaluation. These methods empower predictive analytics and intelligent automation, bridging the gap between data exploration and decision‑making. By applying machine learning, organizations can anticipate customer behavior, optimize operations, and uncover hidden relationships within complex datasets. The ability to design, train, and evaluate models is what transforms static analysis into dynamic, forward‑looking intelligence.

📑 MEAL Report Template – Summary

A MEAL report template provides a structured way to document findings, highlight strengths, and identify areas for improvement. It blends narrative, tables, and visuals to guide evidence-based decision-making.

1. Executive Summary

  • Concise overview of key findings.
  • Highlights of compliance levels, strengths, and improvement areas.
  • Reference to dashboards or visuals for quick insights.

2. Assessment Scope & Methodology

  • Methods: inspections, document reviews, interviews, data analysis.
  • Coverage of locations, programmes, and limitations.

3. School Assessment Findings

  • Data quality scores by location.
  • Compliance with food storage standards.
  • Cooking and serving practices ratings.
  • Trends over time and performance indicators.
  • Monitoring checklist analysis and compliance levels.

4. QA Function Effectiveness & Supplier Checks

  • Effectiveness of QA processes (gauge charts).
  • Supplier warehouse compliance (donut charts).
  • Food production premises inspection results (stacked bar charts).
  • Data quality assessment (pie charts).

5. Issue Resolution & Training

  • Tracking of issues and corrective measures.
  • Summary of training and guidance activities.

6. Recommendations & Follow-Up

  • Actionable recommendations with priorities and responsible parties.
  • Timeline for follow-up actions.

7. Periodic MEAL Status Dashboard

  • Delivery and logistics quality metrics.
  • Stewardship and simplicity indicators.
  • Best practices implementation scores.

8. Conclusion

Reinforces commitment to quality and efficiency, emphasizing continuous improvement and sustainability in programme operations.

Mental Health Risk Modelling

▼

Report Summary

The Mental Health Risk Prediction Report analyzes a dataset of 25,000 records to classify risk levels (Low, Moderate, High).

  • Risk Distribution: 37.4% Low, 47.3% Moderate, 15.3% High
  • Model Performance: Random Forest (AUC 1.00, Accuracy 99%) outperforms Logistic Regression (AUC 0.90, Accuracy 75%)
  • Key Risk Drivers: Depression, anxiety, work stress, poor sleep, substance use, prior mental illness history
  • Protective Factors: Social support, physical activity, sleep hygiene, workplace stress management
  • Implications: Employers and universities should integrate stress management programs; preventive campaigns should emphasize lifestyle changes; health systems should flag individuals with family/medical history.

In short: Random Forest delivers near-perfect classification of mental health risk, while lifestyle and social support remain critical levers for prevention.

Smartphone Addiction: Predictive Analytics and Behavioral Insights

▼

Report Summary

The Smartphone Addiction Report analyzes digital usage, lifestyle patterns, and academic impacts among 7,500 individuals. Using a logistic regression model (GLM), the study identifies screen time, social media engagement, and reduced sleep as the strongest predictors of addiction, with 70.8% of participants classified as addicted. The model achieved 89% accuracy and an AUC of 0.95, confirming high reliability. Findings highlight significant academic disruption, lifestyle risk factors such as sleep deprivation, and the dominance of behavioral variables over demographics in predicting addiction. Strategic recommendations emphasize screen time regulation and sleep hygiene improvement as priority interventions to mitigate risks and improve wellbeing.

Data Quality – Limitations





Key challenges encountered in MoPhones’ datasets:


    
  
  • Missing IDs – Several sheets lack consistent unique identifiers (LoanID/CustomerID), making merges error‑prone and increasing risk of duplicates.
    • 
  
  
  • Too Many Variables – Redundant fields and poorly defined variables add noise, slow analysis, and obscure key indicators.
    • 
  
  
  • Unstructured, Fragmented Data – Credit snapshots, demographics, and NPS survey data are spread across multiple sheets with inconsistent formatting, making integration time‑consuming.
    • 
  
  
  • Ambiguity in Account Status Codes – Overlapping categories (Active, Inactive, PAR 7, PAR 30, Return) are not mutually exclusive, reducing clarity in portfolio monitoring.
    • 







Impact: These limitations slow down monitoring, increase risk of errors, and weaken the reliability of insights drawn from the portfolio data.







  
Recommendations for Improvement





Proposed actions to strengthen MoPhones’ credit data structure:


    
  
  • Introduce Consistent Unique IDs – Standardize LoanID and CustomerID across all datasets to enable seamless merging and reduce duplication.
    • 
  
  
  • Streamline Variables – Audit and remove unnecessary fields, focusing on core metrics such as loan performance, repayment history, arrears status, and NPS etc,.
    • 
  
  
  • Consolidate Data Capture – Replace fragmented sheets with a single relational database or structured workbook using standardized column names and formats.
    • 
  
  
  • Standardize Account Status Definitions – Define clear, mutually exclusive categories (Active, Paid Off, Arrears, Default) and nest PAR flags under arrears for consistency.
    • 







Objective: Build a cleaner, more reliable credit monitoring system that reduces analyst time spent on data preparation and supports better decision‑making.





MoPhones Data Model for Repeatable Reporting



    
  
  • Customer is the central entity, keyed by LoanID, holding demographic and segmentation fields.
    • 
  
  • Sales records transactional data linked to each customer via LoanID.
    • 
  
  • CreditSnapshot provides quarterly portfolio health metrics (balance, arrears, repayment status).
    • 
  
  • NPSResponse captures customer satisfaction and feedback, tied back to LoanID.
    • 
  
  • LoanID serves as the unifying key across all datasets, enabling consistent joins.
    • 
  
  • This structure supports repeatable reporting by aligning demographics, transactions, credit outcomes, and customer experience.
