About Me

Bridging Statistics with Humanitarian Impact

I am a Statistician, Data Analyst, and Monitoring & Evaluation (M&E) professional from Kenya, with a BSc in Statistics (Upper Second Class Honours, 2018–2022) from Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology. My journey into data began earlier at Homa Bay High School (2014–2017), where my interest in mathematics and problem-solving first took shape. Since then, I have pursued a career dedicated to transforming data into meaningful insights that strengthen accountability and improve service delivery.

Over the past three years, I have worked across humanitarian programmes and government projects, applying statistical rigor and analytical creativity to ensure programme quality and evidence-based decision-making. As a programme quality assurance , I focus on building reliable measurement systems and actionable insights. As a data scientist, I embrace curiosity and innovation; designing dashboards, exploring anomalies, and developing predictive models that make complex information accessible. And as a MEAL professional, I recognize that behind every dataset are human lives, and my responsibility is to represent their stories truthfully, ethically, and inclusively.

My path is deeply tied to the future of Kenya and Africa at large, where data-driven solutions are increasingly vital for education, humanitarian response, governance, and sustainable development. I draw inspiration from my spiritual grounding, which reminds me to approach my work with humility, compassion, and service. Professionalism guides me to deliver with precision and reliability, while inclusivity ensures diverse voices are reflected in the systems I help build. For me, data is not just analysis; it is about creating trust, fostering collaboration, and driving measurable impact that can transform communities across the continent.
